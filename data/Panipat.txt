Panipat (pronunciation ) is a historic city in Haryana, India. It is 95 km north of Delhi and 169 km south of Chandigarh on NH-1. The three major battles fought in 1526, 1556 and 1761 took place near the city. The city is famous in India as the "City of Weavers" and "Textile City". It is also known as the "cast-off capital" due to being "the global centre for recycling textiles".. Panipat is included in the list of Critically Polluted Industrial area in India, it has Comprehensive Environment Pollution Index (CEPI) 71.91 as against Ankaleshwar (Gujrat) which is 88.50.
